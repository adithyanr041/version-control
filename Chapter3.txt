2. Behavioral Questions

They’ll want to assess how well you’ll fit in with the team, your ability to learn, and your work ethic.

Common Behavioral Questions:

Tell me about yourself.

Focus on your education, personal projects, and what inspired you to pursue software engineering.

Why did you choose software engineering?

Discuss your passion for solving problems and how technology can make an impact.

Tell me about a time you faced a challenge. How did you overcome it?

You can use the STAR method (Situation, Task, Action, Result) to structure your answer.

How do you stay updated with new technologies?

Mention online courses, reading blogs, participating in hackathons, or contributing to open-source projects.
